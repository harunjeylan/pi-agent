# Domain Transfer Skill

Borrow ideas from other industries to innovate in your domain.

## Concept

> "Invention is connecting creativity with needs" - often the best innovations come from seeing how others solved similar problems.

## How It Works

1. **Identify** your problem/challenge
2. **Discover** how other industries solved similar
3. **Adapt** the idea to your context
4. **Innovate** by combining approaches

## Transfer Process

### Step 1: Define Your Problem
```
I need to solve: [Problem]
In domain: [Your industry]
```

### Step 2: Find Analogous Domains

Ask:
- Who else solves a similar problem?
- What industry has mastered this?
- What's the oldest/coldest solution?

### Step 3: Research Their Solutions

- How did they solve it?
- What made it work?
- What were their failures?
- What's their secret?

### Step 4: Abstract the Pattern

Strip away industry specifics:
```
Pattern: [What's the core idea?]
Why it works: [The principle]
```

### Step 5: Adapt to Your Context

Ask:
- How does this apply here?
- What must change?
- What stays the same?
- What's the adaptation?

### Step 6: Combine & Innovate

- Can we combine multiple patterns?
- What's uniquely possible now?
- What's never been done?

## Example Transfer

**Problem:** User onboarding takes too long, users drop off

**Healthcare Solution:** Clinical trials have rigorous onboarding
- Screening (qualify users)
- Education (teach users)
- Monitoring (track progress)
- Follow-up (re-engage)

**Adapted Pattern:**
- Screening quiz (qualify fit)
- Interactive tutorial (teach)
- Progress tracking (monitor)
- Email/SMS reminders (follow-up)

**Innovated:**
- Users who complete onboarding get "health score"
- Gamify completion with levels
- Social proof: "85% of users complete in 3 days"
